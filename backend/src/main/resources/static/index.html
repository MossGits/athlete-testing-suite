Mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI)

What it is

A mild traumatic brain injury (concussion), is a type of brain injury resulting from a blow, jolt, or a rapid acceleration/deceleration force to the head.

Why it’s missed

Because symptoms vary—and diagnosis often depends on self-reported symptoms—concussions can be overlooked or underreported.

Why it matters

Even a single blow can change brain function, and repeated concussions have been linked to worse long-term outcomes.

The dangers of going undiagnosed

Common consequences

Return-to-play decisions that rely on subjective symptom resolution can lead to premature return and increased risk of certain complications. One of the most severe risks is second impact syndrome—a second head injury before full recovery—which can be fatal within minutes.
