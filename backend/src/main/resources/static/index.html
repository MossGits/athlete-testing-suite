What a concussion is

What it is

A concussion is a brain injury resulting from a blow, jolt, or rapid acceleration or deceleration force to the head.

Why it’s missed

Symptoms vary, and diagnosis often depends on what an athlete feels and reports. That can make concussions easier to overlook or underreport.

Why it matters

Even one blow can affect brain function, and repeated concussions can lead to worse short-term and long-term outcomes.

The danger of going undiagnosed

Why fast screening matters

Return-to-play decisions based only on symptom resolution can lead to premature return and increased risk of serious complications. One of the most severe risks is second impact syndrome, where a second head injury happens before full recovery.
