Mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI) in plain language

What it is

An mTBI (often called a concussion) can happen after a blow or jolt to the head or body that affects brain function. Symptoms may appear immediately or develop over hours.

Why it’s missed

People may “feel fine” at first, minimize symptoms, or not realize that issues like headache, fogginess, irritability, light sensitivity, or sleep changes can be related.

Why it matters

Continuing activity while symptomatic can increase risk—worsening symptoms, prolonged recovery, or additional injury. Early recognition helps guide rest, monitoring, and safe return-to-play decisions.

The dangers of going undiagnosed

Common consequences

  • Returning to play too soon and taking another hit
  • Symptoms lasting longer than expected due to inadequate rest and monitoring
  • School/work performance issues from attention, memory, or sleep disruption
  • Underreporting symptoms because there’s no consistent check-in process

When to seek urgent care

If any of the following are present after a head injury, seek emergency evaluation:

  • Worsening headache, repeated vomiting, or increasing confusion
  • Loss of consciousness, seizure, or severe drowsiness
  • Weakness/numbness, slurred speech, or uneven pupils
  • Neck pain with neurologic symptoms
